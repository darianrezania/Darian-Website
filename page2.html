Professional Focus

One topic I care a lot about is the intersection of neurodegenerative diseases with gene editing. As of now there are very limited treatments regarding neurodegenerative diseases. A new potential game changer is gene editing. Gene editing technologies seem like something from science fiction. Being able to go in take out the bad gene and replace it with the correct one, has the potential to be the cure to many or all diseases. The issue with this is it is a lot harder than it sounds. One of the main issues stopping many academic institutions from using gene editing is the fact that it is very difficult to get the gene editing tools into the brain. Creating a safe and effective delivery system is one of the many challenges facing this field.

Gene editing in the brain has not been successful in human patients yet, but there have been some breakthroughs in other organs. For example there have been some successful gene editing treatments for sickle cell anemia. This is a disease which has been treated via ex vivo gene editing. This means that the patient's cells are taken out of the body, edited, and then put back in. This is a much easier process than in vivo gene editing, which is what would be required for treating neurodegenerative diseases.
